BUG 3: Deeks structural (k=4) — p ∈ (0,1) FAIL
  File:     js/tests.js  (Deeks structural k=4 test block)
  Test:     interceptP ∈ (0,1) for a 4-study dataset
  Cause:    In the Deeks WLS regression, the predictor is xi = 1/√ESS_i and
            the weight is wi = ESS_i. This gives wi·xi² = ESS_i·(1/ESS_i) = 1
            exactly for every study, so Wxx = Σwi·xi² = k (number of studies).
            This algebraic identity makes the 2×2 normal-equation matrix
            ill-conditioned (det ≈ W·k − Wx²), and the Gauss-Jordan inversion
            inside wls() may fail or produce extreme values when the condition
            number is high (~163 for the test data). The p-value is then invalid.
  Fix:      Either use a dataset where ESS values are more spread (improving
            conditioning), or investigate whether wls() needs a fallback for
            near-singular systems in this specific configuration.
